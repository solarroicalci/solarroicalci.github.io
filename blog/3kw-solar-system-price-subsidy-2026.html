3kW Solar System Price in India with Subsidy (2026): PM Surya Ghar Guide

Published: April 2026 | By Solar ROI Calculator Expert Team

With power tariffs expected to increase by 10-15% in 2026, millions of Indian homeowners are actively searching for ways to achieve a zero electricity bill. If your monthly electricity bill ranges between ₹2,500 and ₹4,000, installing a rooftop solar system is no longer just an environmental choice—it is a critical financial investment.

But how much does it actually cost? In this comprehensive 2026 guide, we break down the exact pricing, the latest hardware technologies, and how you can leverage the PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana to drastically reduce your upfront costs.

Why 3kW is the Most Popular Solar System in India

If you look at the residential solar market across India, the 3kW on-grid system is the undisputed best-seller. Why? It comes down to government policy and standard household consumption.

First, a 3kW system generates approximately 12 to 15 units (kWh) of electricity per day. This is the exact amount required to power a standard middle-class Indian home running a refrigerator, washing machine, multiple fans, lights, televisions, and a 1.5-ton inverter AC during the summer. Second, 3kW is the absolute "sweet spot" for government subsidies. It allows you to claim the maximum possible Central Financial Assistance (CFA) before the subsidy cap kicks in.

Breakdown of a 3kW Solar System Price in 2026

The cost of a solar plant depends heavily on the technology you choose. In 2026, older polycrystalline panels are completely obsolete. The market is dominated by high-efficiency TOPCon (Tunnel Oxide Passivated Contact) and N-Type Bifacial panels from top-tier brands like Tata Power, Waaree, and Adani Solar. While slightly more expensive, they generate up to 10% more power and degrade much slower over their 25-year lifespan.

Here is a realistic estimate of the gross costs before subsidies for a premium 3kW system:

  • High-Efficiency Solar Panels (3kW): ₹80,000 - ₹95,000
  • On-Grid Solar Inverter (3kVA - 5kVA): ₹35,000 - ₹45,000
  • Hot-Dip Galvanized Mounting Structure: ₹15,000 - ₹20,000
  • AC/DC Cables, Earthing & Lightning Arrester: ₹15,000
  • Net Metering & Vendor Installation Charges: ₹15,000 - ₹20,000

Total Estimated Gross Cost: ₹1,60,000 to ₹1,95,000

How Much is the PM Surya Ghar Subsidy for 3kW?

The PM Surya Ghar Muft Bijli Yojana makes this transition highly affordable. Under the 2026 guidelines, the central government provides a direct bank transfer to lower your burden.

You receive ₹30,000 per kW for the first 2kW, and an additional ₹18,000 for the third kW. This brings your total guaranteed Central Subsidy to exactly ₹78,000. Any capacity you install above 3kW does not receive additional central funds.

Furthermore, several states offer "Top-Up" subsidies. For example, installing a 3kW system in Uttar Pradesh grants you an additional state subsidy of ₹30,000, bringing your total financial aid to a massive ₹1,08,000!

Best Inverters for a 3kW System in 2026

Your solar panels might be the muscle of the operation, but your inverter is the brain. For a 3kW setup, you need a high-quality string inverter that can handle the high-current output of modern TOPCon panels without clipping the power.

In 2026, brands like Growatt, Solis, and Luminous offer excellent 3.3kW to 5kW single-phase inverters. We highly recommend purchasing an inverter with built-in Wi-Fi and a mobile app. This allows you to track your real-time daily generation and catch any maintenance issues immediately from your phone.

Space Required for a 3kW Solar Plant

A common question homeowners ask is whether their roof is large enough. Because 2026 relies on high-wattage panels (often 500W to 550W per panel), you need fewer physical plates than you did a few years ago. A modern 3kW system requires roughly 250 to 300 square feet of shadow-free roof area. If you are installing on a flat concrete roof, elevated structures can be used so you don't lose your walking or drying space.

Is a 3kW Off-Grid System Subsidized?

This is a critical distinction that catches many buyers off guard. No, Off-Grid systems (systems with battery backups) do not qualify for the PM Surya Ghar subsidy.

The government subsidy is specifically designed to encourage citizens to feed power back into the national grid via Net Metering. If your area experiences frequent, prolonged power cuts and you absolutely must install a lithium or lead-acid battery bank, you will have to bear the full ₹1.6 Lakh to ₹1.9 Lakh gross cost entirely out of pocket.

Can You Run an AC on a 3kW On-Grid System?

Yes, absolutely. However, it is crucial to understand how an on-grid system works. Your solar panels do not run the AC directly. Instead, your panels feed electricity into your house, and whatever you don't use is pushed into the main city grid through your Bi-Directional Net Meter.

When you run your AC at night, you pull electricity back from the grid. At the end of the month, the electricity board calculates the difference. Because a 3kW system generates roughly 360 to 450 units a month, it creates enough "energy credits" during the day to completely offset the heavy electricity usage of a 1.5-ton AC running every night.
